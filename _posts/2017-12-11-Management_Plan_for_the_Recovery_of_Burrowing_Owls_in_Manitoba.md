They are currently still listed as endangered within Manitoba, as well as the rest of Canada. The burrowing owls are also protected under the federal Migratory Birds Convention Act (Government, 2017). The Manitoba Burrowing Owl Recovery Program (MBORP) is a non-profit organization that works towards increasing the population of Burrowing Owls in Manitoba. Each year soft- releases are done with breeding pairs. The recovery program collects research information each year such as productivity relating to clutch size, hatching success, survival of young, foraging of males post-hatching, home range size, diet/prey use, dispersal, mortality, return rates/survival, and other behaviors.
